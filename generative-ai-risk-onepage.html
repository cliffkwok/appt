Generative AI Risk

Poor AI use can turn useful data into exposed data.

The risk is rarely the model alone. It comes from unsafe inputs, weak review, and public outputs that can be copied, mined, or reconstructed.

Input

Sensitive internal data

Meeting notes, source code, customer files, contracts, research, pricing, strategy, or unreleased plans.

Unsafe AI Workflow

Four common mistakes create the leak path.

Paste raw data Confidential files go into public or unmanaged AI tools.
No review step Generated output is shared before facts, sources, and privacy are checked.
Train or test casually Internal examples become prompts, fine-tuning data, or reusable context.
Public sharing AI-written content reveals details that competitors or attackers can mine.
Impact
!

Data leakage becomes business risk

Core data, IP, or source code can be exposed.
False or sensitive AI output can spread publicly.
Attackers can reconstruct context from repeated prompts.
Trust, compliance, and market position can be damaged.
Minimize inputRemove secrets before prompting.
Use approved toolsPrefer enterprise AI with data controls.
Human reviewCheck privacy, accuracy, and ownership.
Log and classifyKnow what data entered which model.

Simple rule: if the data cannot be posted in a public channel, do not paste it into an unmanaged AI tool.
